# Continuity Hypothesis (Experimental)

## Motivation
This branch explores whether continuity of consciousness and identity can be framed
as a physical question grounded in information, organization, and dynamics,
rather than metaphysical assertion.

The goal is not to prove survival after death, but to test whether identifiable
informational organization exhibits graded persistence, degradation, or recoverability
under lawful conditions.

## Hypothesis
Consciousness corresponds to a dynamically stable, self-referential informational pattern
implemented in physical systems. While the substrate supporting this pattern may degrade
or cease, the information defining the pattern is not fundamentally destroyed but redistributed.

Continuity, if it exists, is therefore graded rather than binary, and depends on the degree
to which identifiable informational structure remains coherent and recoverable over time.

## What This Does Not Claim
- No claim of personal immortality
- No claim of guaranteed post-death recognition
- No supernatural assumptions
- No metaphysical certainty

This is an experimental framing only.

## Experimental Direction
We test continuity via:
- informational integration
- recurrence / stability
- causal feedback
- robustness under perturbation

Failure of these signatures falsifies the hypothesis.
